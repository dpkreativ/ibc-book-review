Sweet Medicine
by Panashe Chigumadzi

Set in Harare at the height of Zimbabwe's economic woes in 2008, Sweet Medicine tells the story of Tsitsi, a young woman who seeks agency and financial security through a clear-eyed (some might say cynical) reliance on patriarchy and the spiritual world. It explores the breakdown of the middle class, the turn to traditional/spiritual solutions for modern problems, and the lengths one will go to survive.
